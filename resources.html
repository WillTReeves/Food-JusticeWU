Food Resources

Feeding America

Feeding America is a national organization working to end food insecurity in the United States while combating food waste. Feeding America has programs to reach children, seniors, and families no matter where they live or spend time. Last year, the Feeding America network and their partners rescued 4.7 billion pounds of groceries. Their network of food banks reaches every county in the nation making them uniquely prepared to provide food to areas dealing with natural disasters. They are also one of the largest organization responsible for researching hunger in the United States.

If you would like to help Feeding America in their mission you can get involved under the take action page of their website.

Save The Food

Save The Food is a national organization working to end food waste across America. They are attempting to do this by educating consumers on how to plan cook and store food in the best possible ways.

FindHelp.org

FindHelp.org connects people to social care services near them. They work with thousands of organizations all over the country to maximize their impact. They have resources that help provide food, housing, health, legal help, employment support, counseling and much more. They also offer education and training resources for people who want to get more involved.

Oregon Food Bank

Oregon Food Bank and its statewide partner network work to help people find free and nutritious food in communities throughout Oregon and Southwest Washington. They offer many resources for food-insecure people including free groceries, nutritious meal delivery and fresh produce. They also have programs with more tailored support which provide food to pregnant women and families with young children. They also provide SNAP which gives benefit cards to people in need.

If you would like to support Oregon food bank they have a tab on their website where you can donate, volunteer or get involved in their systemic change programs.

OHSU Food RX

The OHSU Food Rx program was developed by OHSU Food and Nutrition Services to give clinicians an opportunity to offer patients a tangible solution to their food insecurity in the form of vouchers that can be redeemed for fruits and vegetables at one of 32 participating farmers markets and grocery stores in Oregon. programs choose to offer each patient around $30 per month, usually for 2-3 months. Currently, no clinics are running this program, but the system is in place to do it if they do adopt it.

Street Roots Rose City

Street Roots is the publisher of the Rose City Resource, the most comprehensive list of services for people experiencing homelessness and poverty in Clackamas, Multnomah, and Washington counties. There is an online version of the booklet available, but they are also available in person at their street roots office.

Free Fridge Salem

Free Fridge Salem is a local mutual aid project started to help our neighbors with food insecurity and basic human needs without strings attached. In addition to food, they provide personal hygiene products, harm reduction supplies, and seasonal aid offerings. Their locations and events are all available on their website.

If you would like to support Free Fridge Salem they need volunteers to help work at their warehouse you can sign up on their website.

Family Kitchen

The family kitchen works to serve anyone who needs nutritious meals in a safe and caring environment. They provide over 2,000 fresh to-go meals. Due to covid they are unable to provide in person meals but plan to move back eventually.

Bearcat Pantry

The Bearcat pantry is a part of Willamette Universities SOAR Center and works to provide resources to students who identify as food insecure. The pantry has a large selection of food including fruits and vegetables (fresh, frozen, and canned), proteins, dairy, grains, and other staples. They also keep in stock non-food items like Tupperware and feminine hygiene. The pantry is free to all Willamette Students and is run by students and volunteers. They are located on the third floor of the UC and their hours are available on Their website

If you would like to support the Bearcat pantry they accept food, monetary donations, and meal points, there is information
about how to donate on their website.
